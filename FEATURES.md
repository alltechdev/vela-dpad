## Map & rendering
- ✅ **Settings reorganized + navigation UI refresh.** Sections follow usage order: Appearance, Map
  (traffic, transit lines, 3D buildings), **Place pages** (Show reviews, the "Read all reviews" button,
  Load photos), Navigation, Voice, Offline, then the rest. The four stacked vibrate-on-turns switches
  became one row of chips, one per travel mode. In-drive UI: maneuver banner + bottom bar are soft-cornered
  floating cards with shadows, a bigger turn glyph, bolder distance, heavier road name, and filled tonal
  buttons instead of thin outlined circles.
- ✅ **Arrive step names the destination.** The ARRIVE step in the banner, step list and arrival card shows
  the business name and address under "Arrive at your destination", Google-style. Robust to partial data:
  name, else address, else raw coordinates — no line repeats another (an address search's "name" IS its
  address, so it prints once). `NavSession.destinationDisplay` (pure, unit-tested `DestinationDisplayTest`);
  address rides `NavSession.State.destinationAddress` → `navDestAddress`.
- ✅ **Zoomed-in pan smoothness.** Three trims: (1) the scale bar recomposes only when its value changes over
  1%, not every pan frame; (2) house numbers (basemap layer + streamed overlay) no longer occupy the symbol
  collision index (`textIgnorePlacement`) — they still yield to icons/labels but cost the placement pass
  almost nothing and can never evict a business icon; (3) a **Settings → Map → 3D buildings** toggle (on by
  default) controls the z16+ fill-extrusion layer — the most fragment-expensive thing the map draws, so off
  is the one-tap fix on weaker GPUs; flat footprints stay either way.
- ✅ **Highlight transit lines (train + subway).** Settings → Map → **Highlight transit lines** draws rail in colour, Google-transit-layer style: heavy rail purple, subway/light-rail/tram teal. Data is already in the keyless OpenFreeMap basemap tiles (OpenMapTiles `transportation` source-layer, `class` = rail / transit), so it's just a coloured `LineLayer` over existing tiles, **no new data source, no network** (`ensureTransit` in `VelaMapView`, width zoom-interpolated, inserted below the first symbol layer so station/road labels stay on top). **ON by default**, persisted (`TransitLayer` holder). Removes cleanly when off; no-op on a non-OpenMapTiles basemap. Localized in all 11 languages. *(Station markers are a possible follow-up.)*
- ✅ **Build a route between two points on the map ("Choose on map").** The origin/stop picker has a **Choose on map** row: tapping it leaves the search overlay and shows the live map with a **centre crosshair**, a hint banner, and a **Set start / Set stop** button. Move the map under the crosshair and confirm, **or long-press** anywhere to set the point directly. The picked point is reverse-geocoded (falls back to a bare pin) and the route recomputes. `MapPick` enum + `pickOnMap` state; `chooseOriginOnMap`/`chooseStopOnMap`/`confirmMapPick`/`cancelChooseOnMap` in the VM; `onMapLongPress` honors the mode; the directions panel / place sheet / top search bar hide while the crosshair is up, and system-back cancels it. Localized in all 11 languages.
- ✅ **Camera stays put when you close a place sheet or exit Settings.** Two causes. (1) The bottom-inset handler now only re-frames when the sheet APPEARS or grows, never when it closes (closing nulled `lastCameraTarget` in `VelaMapView` and let the fall-through camera branch re-center on the stale `state.center` at browse zoom). (2) Settings now draws as an opaque overlay ON TOP of a still-composed `MapScreen`, so the MapLibre `MapView` (and its camera/zoom) survives the round trip instead of being disposed and re-seeded from the stale center. **Follow-on:** `clearSelection` refreshes the ambient POIs for the current view, so they come back when you close a place.
- ✅ MapLibre Native vector rendering (Compose-wrapped)
- ✅ Detailed open basemap: bundled OpenFreeMap Liberty + injected house numbers at z17; OpenMapTiles vector source pinned to OpenFreeMap's **versioned** tile path (the un-versioned path serves empty tiles)
- ✅ Route line, **tappable Google-style search-result pins**, location dot as GeoJSON layers
- ✅ Heading-up, tilted navigation camera; fit-route-to-screen on preview; recenter FAB. **Pinch-zoom during nav keeps the follow-camera:** a pinch keeps tracking your position at the zoom you chose (a dedicated `OnScaleListener` adopts it as an override and suppresses auto-zoom while you pinch; captured continuously during the pinch, cleared only on a **real pan** or at **nav-end**, keyed on `navMode`), while a **pan** detaches to let you look around (`OnMoveListener`) and **Re-center** clears the override back to auto-zoom + follow. Pan-detach fires from `onMove` (not `onMoveBegin`, which could precede `onScaleBegin` and misread a pinch as a pan), so a pinch locks your zoom AND keeps tracking and only a real pan detaches. The camera follows `navPuck.drawn` directly (predictive framing was tried + reverted — it stuttered on real GPS). **Per-frame follow:** the follow-camera runs in the **motion ticker at 60 fps**, easing toward the smoothed puck point each frame (~0.12 s), seeded from the live camera on (re)attach; the old throttled block (>4 m / >2° with a 550 ms ease) is kept only for the pre-engage / off-route case. **Speed-adaptive zoom** (Google-style: pulls back on the freeway, tightens on city streets) is continuous + low-passed over a damped speed (hard speed-band thresholds ping-ponged the zoom in stop-and-go). During nav it follows the **smoothed motion-model puck point** (see the location-indicator entry below), not the raw GPS fix, so the view can't lurch when the snap is briefly ambiguous
- ✅ **Traversed route greys out** behind the vehicle (Google-style) — grey for the part driven, live traffic ahead. **During nav the route line carries traffic per segment, not the whole map** — the `/maps/vt` whole-map overlay no longer auto-shows when navigating (it washed every road; the ask was traffic on "just the road we're on"). The route is coloured **per segment** — free-flow blue with amber/red bands over congested stretches (from `route[3][5][0]`; see "Routing & traffic"). The manual traffic toggle still paints the full map. Nav-overlay polish: speedometer + recenter lifted clear of the bottom bar, recenter is an icon-only FAB bottom-right. **Maneuver-banner swipe:** `rememberUpdatedState` makes each swipe walk to the next/prev step (`pointerInput(Unit)` had captured a stale step index, repeating one card)
- ✅ Compass kept clear of the status bar (inset-aware margins)
- ✅ Tap a labelled POI **or a search-result pin** to open it; camera frames all results after a search. Tapping a POI reads `name:latin`/`name:en` (not just `name`), and an **unnamed** POI icon **reverse-geocodes to a pin + address** instead of being a dead tap. When several Google listings share a spot (e.g. a co-branded "SpeeDee Midas" with a sparse **Midas** and a rich **SpeeDee** profile), the tap opens the **most-reviewed = canonical** one, not the nearest
- ✅ Bottom sheets (place sheet, steps) **fill to the screen edge** — content is padded off the gesture/nav bar, but the sheet background no longer stops short and lets the map peek through at the bottom
- ✅ **Tap a house number or a building to open its address.** A single tap on a **house-number label** (the map's `addr:housenumber` or the streamed address overlay) opens a pin + place sheet **snapped to that exact number** — tapping "1020" gives *1020 Olive Dr*, not a neighbour. Google's reverse-geocode snaps to the nearest addressable point and routinely returns a different house, so the tap LEADS with the number on the label and uses the geocode only for the street/city. A tap on a plain **building footprint** (no number showing) reverse-geocodes it the same way. A real business on that spot opens as the business. Empty land has no footprint, so a tap there does nothing and only a **long-press** drops a raw pin (below)
- ✅ **Long-press the map** → drop a pin, reverse-geocode it (Nominatim/OSM, keyless), then get Directions — works even where no building is drawn. When the point **doesn't snap to a street address** (bare road, open land, or a failed geocode) the sheet surfaces the **lat/lng coordinates prominently** (a `MyLocation`-iconed row, tappable to copy) beside the road name. A house-numbered snap ("1024 Olive Dr") or a real business POI shows its address instead; the snap is detected by the name's first token being a pure-digit house number (a numbered street like "120th St" keeps its "th", so it reads as unsnapped → coordinates)

- ✅ **In-app Light / Dark / Follow-system switch** (Settings → Appearance) — sets Vela's theme **independently of the phone** (the whole app + the map recolour live off one preference, `AppTheme`/`isAppInDarkTheme`; persisted). Dark mode recolours **every** landuse/landcover fill, not just a hardcoded few, so no light/cream patches break the night palette
- ✅ **Google-style POI markers** — category-coloured circles with white Material Icons glyphs (food=orange, shop=blue, park=green, health=red, transit=blue, …), generated at runtime over a bundled Material Icons font, `iconSize` 0.8; in light mode the POI **label text is coloured by category too**. **Density tuned for parity:** Liberty's always-on `poi_transit` layer is pushed to **z16+**, while the next business tier (`poi_r7`) is pulled to **z15** so more shops/restaurants show. **Nameless POIs are filtered out** (`has("name")` AND-ed onto each poi layer's rank filter) — they couldn't be opened anyway. **Ranked by prominence, not tile order:** when two OSM POIs collide, each poi layer sets `symbol-sort-key = rank` (lower wins). *(The map's business POIs are Google's, not OSM's — see below; this only affects the OSM fallback.)* **Marker style — grey teardrop pin + left-aligned labels, no white ring:** markers sit in front of a muted-grey TEARDROP/pin backing (Google grey `#9AA0A6`) with a soft drop shadow (`PoiIcons.marker`: a `Path` teardrop = body circle unioned with a tangent-sided triangle to the tip + `BlurMaskFilter`; the coloured dot is the bitmap centre so the default centre-anchor keeps the dot on the place). The **label sits to the LEFT of the icon** — `textAnchor` RIGHT + a negative em `textOffset` (scales with the prominence-driven text size), on both the ambient Google POI layer (`VelaMapView`) and the OSM `poi_r*`/`poi_transit` fallback layers (`PoiIcons.applyToLiberty`). **Labels are UPRIGHT everywhere:** `applyToLiberty` pins **`Noto Sans Regular`** on the OSM layers too (Liberty's default POI face is *Italic*, which slanted the labels the moment ambient cleared — search/selected place/nav).
- ✅ **Building footprints + house numbers, Google-style.** The bundled Liberty `building` FILL layer is `minzoom 13 / maxzoom 14`, and MapLibre `maxzoom` is **exclusive**, so `setMinZoom(14f)` alone collapsed its range to empty (`14 ≤ z < 14`) and the flat footprints never painted. Fixed with **`setMaxZoom(24f)`** on the `building` layer in both `applyLight`/`applyDark`, so flat grey footprints with a crisp darker outline draw from ~z14 up; `building-3d` extrusions are **gated to z16+** (extrusion is the per-pixel-expensive part on a Pixel 5a). **House numbers**: the `vela-housenumber` layer (OMT `housenumber` source-layer) renders where OSM has `addr:housenumber` (OpenFreeMap serves that source-layer, verified vs the live TileJSON + z14 tiles); `minZoom` 16. **Coverage caveat (OSM, not the renderer):** footprints are near-complete in US/EU metros but sparse in some newer suburbs, and `addr:housenumber` is patchy — Vela renders whatever OSM has.
- ✅ **Traffic lights + stop signs on the map.** OSM `highway=traffic_signals` draw as a small stoplight icon and `highway=stop` as a red **STOP** octagon, beneath the POI dots + pins, at close zoom (z ≥ 16). Zoom-scaled icon size + draw-all (allowOverlap) so they're visible on the browse map, not culled by the denser POI layer. Keyless (Overpass), fetched per-viewport but **area-cached** — the fetch box is padded 50% beyond the view and reused while you stay inside it. Zoom-gated (no setting); shows in browse and nav.
- ✅ **Map POIs are Google-ranked by real prominence, not OSM.** The map's business POIs come from **ambient Google places** (`nearbyPlaces` — a category fan-out over the viewport); OSM's `poi_r1/r7/r20` are hidden while those show. Deduped Google places are ranked by **real prominence** — `ambientProminence = ln(reviewCount+1) · (0.6 + rating/10)` (review count dominates, rating nudges) — so an anchor store (Safeway, ~1,300 reviews) beats its in-store tenant, and top POIs are recognizable landmarks, not the 0-review junk the fan-out drags in. Pure prominence, exact distance only as a tiebreak. Unit-tested (`AmbientRankingTest`); `rankAmbientPlaces`/`ambientProminence` are pure `:core`. **Deeper + broader + zoom-scaled:** (1) **broader categories** — the fan-out is 13 terms incl. `grocery store / gas station / gym / bar / pharmacy` for a real MIX; (2) **deeper pool** — `!7i40 → !7i60` per term took the candidate pool from ~150 to ~690; (3) the map is handed the POIs **near the view** (`viewRadius × 1.25` filter, `AMBIENT_ONSCREEN_CAP = 140` by prominence), not the whole ~3.5 km pool — off-screen POIs can't render and MapLibre re-runs symbol collision over every handed feature each drag frame, so a large cap cut a low-commercial view's own nearby shops. Low-signal fills leftover collision gaps only. **Perf:** view-filter + cap → 1.38 % janky frames, 90th-pct 15 ms on a Pixel 5a. *(Keyless ceiling: the viewport query floors at a ~3.5 km span, so a hyper-dense downtown at max zoom is bounded by what one span returns.)* **Prominence sizing + smoother panning:** (1) each ambient dot carries its prominence to the layer (`MapMarker.prominence`), driving a data-driven `iconSize` (~0.78 low-signal → ~1.3 for a Safeway/mall) and `textSize` (11→14pt) via MapLibre `interpolate`, plus a prominence-weighted keep-radius (`viewRadius × (1.25 … 1.6)`) so a high-prominence anchor survives off-centre. (2) `applyData` short-circuits when the marker/ambient lists are unchanged (structural equality, reset on style reload) instead of re-`setGeoJson`-ing every recompose; with a lighter halo (0.9) + icon-padding (1.5), drag jank went 1.38 % → 0.47 % janky frames (90th-pct 15 → 10 ms).
- ✅ **Per-travel-mode "Vibrate on turns".** The single haptics toggle became **four** — Driving / Walking / Cycling / Transit. `Haptics.cue(type, approaching, mode)` checks the per-mode pref (`haptics_<mode>`). **Default = on for walk/bike/transit, OFF for driving** (`Haptics.defaultFor`); an existing legacy `haptics_on=false` still wins.
- ✅ **Place-sheet header + actions, Google-style.** **Header:** name + **Save (★) · Share · ⋮ · ✕** as compact 40dp icon buttons; the name is `titleLarge` (22sp) `maxLines=2`. **Action pills:** a highlighted **Directions** + short **Call / Website** (`ActionPill`, horizontally-scrollable). **Phone + website live as their own tappable rows below the address**. The collapsed **Hours** row uses a fixed-width label with the summary stripped to just the hours (`substringBefore("·")`) + ellipsised, so a long holiday value can't squeeze the label. Review-loading copy is "Gathering reviews…".
- ✅ **Search targets the panned viewport, not GPS.** A search biases to the **map centre you're looking at** (`mapCenter ?: myLocation`), so panning to another neighbourhood/city then searching returns results *there*. Autocomplete suggestions do the same. (The "Search this area" button is unchanged.)
- ✅ **Faster POI loading via HTTP concurrency.** The ambient-POI load fires ~13 parallel `google.com` category queries; OkHttp's default 5-requests-per-host serialised them into ~3 rounds (the "POIs take ~10 s" report on slow links). Raised `Dispatcher.maxRequestsPerHost` to 24 so they fire in one round, + a 12 s `callTimeout` so one hung scrape can't stall the fan-out. (~2 s per fetch on wifi is Google's own keyless response latency, the floor.) **Multi-area LRU cache:** the ambient cache is a **16-entry LRU with a 30-min TTL**, so revisiting any recently-viewed area repaints instantly and the TTL stops a since-closed shop lingering all session.
- ✅ **POI dots reappear instantly on zoom-back.** The last fetch is **cached** (`ambientCache`) and repainted immediately (re-centred + view-filtered) when you return to a nearby area, so there's no empty→OSM-flash→ambient "small then pop"; the network fetch then refines it. Settle debounce dropped 500 → 300 ms.
- ✅ **Voice settings decluttered + default speed 0.8×.** Settings → Voice shows just the essentials (pick voice · Test voice · System voice settings), with the playground, speed stepper and variant picker behind a collapsed **"Advanced voice options"** header. Default spoken-directions speed is **0.8×** (compiled + calibration v13). Review-loading copy is "**Gathering reviews…**" / "Reviews · N of ~M".
- ✅ **Replay uses the selected voice, not the system fallback.** Replay wires the neural synth and passes `selectedEngine.packageName`, so it speaks through the same Vela voice as a live drive (`replayTrip` used to start nav with a `null` voice engine → the "system voice at 0.8×" bug). **During turn-by-turn ALL POI tiers are hidden** (`poi_r1`/`poi_r7`/`poi_r20`/`poi_transit` → off while navigating, restored on exit, keyed on the style so a dark/light flip re-applies): POI labels re-ran MapLibre's symbol collision on every nav camera rotate/zoom and popped in/out at zoom thresholds. Google declutters its map the same way during nav.
- ✅ **Buildings** (footprints + 3-D massing) — OSM building data already in the Liberty tiles (`building` 2-D fill + `building-3d` extrusion at high zoom), given enough contrast + a subtle outline, with the 2-D `building` layer's minzoom lowered to 14 so **residential houses surface a zoom-level earlier** (~z17). **Keyless, no API key, no new data/infra.** *Two distinct gaps:* (1) OpenFreeMap's keyless tiles drop small footprints from low-zoom tiles, so houses only appear zoomed-in; (2) **some US residential houses are simply absent from OSM** and render at *no* zoom. Lowering minzoom fixes (1); only **richer building data** closes (2). **Gap (2) is CLOSED for downloaded regions:** the Microsoft-footprint **building overlay** (see Offline) is baked into PMTiles by CI and rendered beneath OSM, so a suburb OSM never mapped (e.g. the test region) fills with real house footprints.
- ✅ **Terrain relief (hillshade)** — Google-style shaded relief from the **keyless
  open terrarium DEM** (AWS Open Data, no key, native fetch so no CORS), added at
  runtime under the road layers (so roads + labels stay crisp) and capped at z16

- ✅ **Ambient Google POIs on the map** — on a bare, zoomed-in browse map, the visible area's prominent **Google** places are fetched automatically and drawn as **small Google-style category dots** (the same `vela-poi-<group>` icons as the OSM POIs, with decluttered labels), so they read as native map POIs, not clashing red pins. Tap to open like any result. **Google-first balance:** while ambient POIs show, the OSM **business** POI layers (`poi_r1/r7/r20`) are hidden; OSM transit + the whole OSM basemap stay, and the OSM POIs come back when ambient is off (zoomed out / offline / nav / search). **Tightly gated:** zoom ≥ 14, bare map only, debounced 500 ms, skipped for pans under 250 m. ## Search & POIs (live Google data)
- ✅ **Toggles to hide reviews and skip photo loading.** Settings → Map, two switches, both on by default. **Show reviews** off = the place sheet renders no review section (no tab, no featured quote, no "Read all reviews") and Vela never runs the review scrape. **Load photos** off = no hero strip or gallery, the WebView photo fetch never starts, and the search-response preview photo is hidden too (a data saver). Both persist (`ShowReviews` / `LoadPhotos` holders) and gate BOTH the fetch in the ViewModel and the render in the place sheet, so off means no traffic. Localized in all 11 languages.
- ✅ **"Hide adult categories" toggle.** Settings → Map, **off by default**. On = drops places whose Google CATEGORY is adult / nightlife / alcohol / gambling / smoking from **both** search results and the ambient map. Matching is on the free-text category **only, never the name**, and PRECISE — food "…bar" categories (sushi/juice/coffee/salad bar) stay. Because Google returns the category **localized** (`hl=<lang>`), the keyword list carries the equivalent terms for all 11 UI languages. Pure `:core` `CategoryFilter` (unit-tested) applied at the data-source seam (`GoogleMapsDataSource.search` + `nearbyPlaces`); gated by a `:core`-visible `enabled` flag the `HideAdult` holder flips. Localized in all 11 languages.
- ✅ **"Hide website & external links" toggle.** Settings → Map, **off by default**. On = place pages don't show the **Website** pill/row, the **Street View** pano, or the **Book / Reserve / Order** action. Internal actions (dial, directions, share a `geo:` pin) are unaffected. Plain `HideExternalLinks` holder (`ui/PlaceContent.kt`, same shape as `ShowReviews`/`LoadPhotos`). Localized in all 11 languages. (Adapted from a community PR; the PR's separate "restricted" build flavor was not taken.)
- ✅ Place search — name, category, **full address (street, city, state, ZIP)**, rating, review count, coordinates
- ✅ Searching a **specific/far address** resolves to that single geocoded location (handles the response's single-result shape, not just the POI list — fixes the old "calibration error" on far addresses); genuinely-empty searches now show "no results" instead of an error
- ✅ **Address → business snap** — searching a raw address that *is* a business (e.g. "1020 Olive Dr, Davis") lands on the **business** (In-N-Out Burger, rating/hours/category), not the bare address — Google lists the "at this place" business under the geocoded node (`[0][1][0][14][68]`) and Vela reads it. *(unit-tested; the path is in calibration so it's remotely fixable)*

- ✅ Search-result rows show **5-star rating**, colour-coded open/closed status, and the **full address (city/state/ZIP)** to disambiguate similar names — **sized for legibility** (name at titleMedium, the rating/category/address lines bumped up)

Works from the handle or anywhere on the body (a nested-scroll handler; mid-list it just scrolls). ## Location (degoogled)
- ✅ AOSP `LocationManager` (GPS + NETWORK), no Fused/GMS
- ✅ Last-known seeding for instant map; PSDS slow-fix tip
- ✅ **Google-style location indicator** — two modes. **Browse:** a blue dot (Google's `#4285F4`) with a white ring + a translucent heading cone/beam beneath the dot — points the **device-facing compass** (the `TYPE_ROTATION_VECTOR` sensor via `HeadingProvider`, low-pass smoothed along the shortest arc, OsmAnd-style) so it shows which way you're facing **even standing still**; falls back to GPS course when the sensor's absent, hidden when neither is available. *(Navigation never uses it — there the heading comes from the matched road; the sensor heading is pushed to state only in browse and only on a real change.)* The dot **greys out when the fix is stale** (~12 s, or before the first live fix) and blue again on a fresh fix.
  - **Nav: a solid blue arrow puck** (the dot is hidden) that **snaps onto the route line** and faces down the road, so lateral GPS jitter doesn't make it jump. The snap is **honest**: it engages only within ~22 m (≈ a road width + GPS error) **and** heading the road's way (±55°); a missed exit / off-road / wrong-way fails the gate and shows the **real** position (then the off-route reroute kicks in).
  - The puck rides a **Google-style forward-progress motion model** instead of teleporting to each raw fix: a per-frame ticker glides it **monotonically forward along the route** with **dead reckoning** (between the ~1 Hz fixes the position keeps advancing), **eased** progress (never jitters backward) and **smoothed heading** (rotates through bends). **Accelerometer-fused speed:** a **1-D Kalman filter** (`core/location/SpeedKalman`, unit-tested) fuses the GPS speed (measurement, each fix) with the **measured forward acceleration** (prediction, every frame — `TYPE_LINEAR_ACCELERATION` rotated into the world frame by `MotionProvider` and projected onto the travel bearing), and the reckoned advance is the **integral of the modelled speed** (∫v·dt, blind-capped at 2 s), so braking collapses the predicted speed NOW. No accelerometer → a = 0 → the old constant-speed behaviour. The speed-adaptive zoom rides the same filtered speed.
  - The on-route match is **forward-only and monotonic** (OsmAnd's `RoutingHelper`): once engaged it searches a **bounded look-ahead AHEAD of current progress only** (`snapToRouteWindowed`, from `targetM − 25 m` to `targetM + speed·8 s` clamped 150–600 m), so a route that **passes near itself** (switchback / cloverleaf / parallel return leg) can't pull the puck onto a far or earlier leg. When nothing's ahead within tolerance it **HOLDS and dead-reckons forward instead of re-snapping globally** (the old global fall-back teleported the camera to a random spot), and only a sustained run of misses (~6 fixes) disengages to re-acquire while `NavEngine`'s off-route logic drives the reroute. The one global search left is the **initial acquisition** at nav-start / just after a reroute.
  - **The follow-camera tracks this same smoothed point** — not the raw fix — so puck and map move as one; **off-route it holds the last road-aligned heading** instead of spinning to the raw GPS bearing. The **traversed-grey split rides the puck's DRAWN position (`progressM`)**, drawn as a **hard `step` at the EXACT fraction** (not quantized to a 256-sample grid, which left the boundary up to route-length/256 m ahead of the arrow), refreshed in the 60 fps motion ticker (throttled to ~3 m of progress).
  - **Heading & speed are synthesised from movement when a GPS fix doesn't carry them** (cold start, some chipsets/ROMs) — gated on real movement so a standstill's jitter can't spin the marker. **A single-fix speed SPIKE is rejected** (a car can't gain >15 m/s between fixes, so the prior speed is kept), in **both live and replay paths**. **Position OUTLIERS are rejected the same way** (`sanePosition`): a coarse NETWORK / multipath fix that leaps farther than is physically plausible is dropped, and the position runs through a **speed-adaptive low-pass** — heavy smoothing at low speed so parked/idle jitter barely nudges the dot, easing to a 1:1 follow by ~10 m/s. Google smooths position this way; OsmAnd does NOT (it only Kalman-smooths heading), so this is steadier at idle. The **first fix of a session anchors** and a leap that **persists ≥2 fixes re-anchors** (without it, a replay starting away from your live position rejected every fix as an outlier and the dot never moved).
- 🟡 BeaconDB WiFi positioning — NETWORK-provider coarse fixes are already used for the browse dot when GPS has been quiet (never during nav); an explicit opt-in and any deeper use are still open

## Platform & distribution
- ✅ **In-app updater.** The PipePipe pattern: Vela checks the newest GitHub release (about once a day on launch, or on demand from Settings → Version → Check for updates), and when the release is newer than the running build a card on the map offers it. Update downloads the APK with a progress bar and hands it to Android's normal package installer, which verifies it is the same app signed with the same key before installing. "Not now" silences that version until a newer one appears. The launch check is a Settings toggle (on by default); Obtainium users can turn it off. Localized.
- ✅ No Google Play Services anywhere
- ✅ Material 3 Compose UI; Hilt DI; R8 release builds
- ✅ Public GitHub repo + local mirror + offline bundle
- ✅ CI (GitHub Actions): every push to main builds + tests + signs the APK and publishes a **nightly prerelease** (`v0.3.<run>`, versionCode `2000+run`); a **weekly job promotes the newest nightly to the stable release** (same APK, no rebuild). Obtainium tracks stable with zero config; nightly users flip on "include prereleases". The in-app updater follows stable
- ✅ **Opt-in diagnostics / debug export** (Settings → Diagnostics, **off by default**) — a local-only event log (searches, computed routes, parser "drift", nav start/reroute/arrival) that the user can **Export debug session** to a JSON bundle and hand to a developer via the system share sheet. **Never auto-uploaded**; turning it off wipes the log; in-memory only (capped at 300 events). `core/diag/DiagLog` + `app/diag/DiagExporter`, consent dialog on enable, `PRIVACY.md` updated
- ✅ **Crash capture** — an uncaught-exception handler (`app/diag/CrashCatcher`, installed in `VelaApp`) **persists the stack trace + breadcrumbs + app/device versions to disk**, surviving the restart, so after a crash the user can **Export crash report** from Settings → Diagnostics. Captured even with diagnostics off (a stack trace is benign + local); never auto-sent; chains to the system handler so normal crash behaviour is unchanged
- ✅ **Trip recording + replay** (Settings → "Save my trips", **off by default, separate opt-in** — more invasive than diagnostics since it's your exact routes; never uploaded) — records each navigation's GPS trace to a local file (`app/replay/TripStore`), so a drive can be **replayed on the map at 3×** to test turn-by-turn (`LocationProvider.replay` feeds the recorded fixes through the same nav/camera/dot pipeline). A replay shows a **"Stop replay"** button and, when it finishes or is stopped, **live GPS resumes automatically**. The trip is **saved the instant you arrive**, not only when you tap "Done". Recorded trips are listed in Settings (newest first) with their **recorded date + point count** and **Replay / Share / Delete**. **Share** exports the raw CSV trace via the system sheet (same FileProvider as the diag/saved-places export) — so a drive can be pulled off a **release** build and handed over for replay/debug **without a dev build**. The **first-run prompt offers the two opt-ins separately** (diagnostics default-on, trip-saving default-off). Replay **auto-routes to the trip's destination** and starts turn-by-turn (best-effort; the auto-started nav is torn down when the replay ends). *(Bigger picture — opt-in **encrypted** upload of traces for remote debugging is the natural next step; see ROADMAP.)*
- ✅ **Simulate driving (demo mode)** (Settings → Navigation → "Simulate driving (demo)", **off by default**) — tapping **Start** on any planned route drives it as a **synthetic GPS trace** instead of using the real fix, so **navigation runs anywhere** — for demos, screenshots and testing turn-by-turn. It reuses the recorded-trip **replay pipeline wholesale**: `DemoTrace.fromRoute` (pure `:core`) walks the route polyline at a constant cruise speed emitting one clean `ReplayFix`/sec, and `LocationProvider.replay` feeds those through the **exact** live-nav loop (hermetic `NavSession.replayMode`, puck physics, follow-camera, spoken + haptic guidance, speed-limit badge). Unlike a recorded trace it's meant to **look like real nav** — no "Stop replay" pill; the normal **End** button stops it (`stopNav` cancels the demo job and resumes live GPS). **Turn it off to navigate for real.**
- ✅ **Simulate my location (demo mode)** (Settings → Navigation → "Simulate my location (demo)", **off by default**) — a sibling of simulate-driving for the *browse* map: Vela pretends you're standing at the current map centre, so the location dot, the directions origin ("Your location"), and recenter all read from there instead of your real GPS. `ui/SimLocation.kt`, a process-wide reactive holder persisted to `vela_settings`; `MapViewModel` pins `myLocation` to the captured point and suspends the live GPS collector, resuming it when you turn the toggle back off. **Turn it off to use real GPS.**

## Resilience / maintainability
- ✅ **Remotely-updatable scraper calibration** — `calibration.json` at the repo
  root holds the `pb` templates, endpoint URLs **and (phase 2) the search parser's
  positional field-index paths** (`name`, `address`, `rating`, `photos`, … as
  `[i,j,…]` arrays). The app fetches it at launch and adopts a newer `version`
  (host-allowlisted to google.com) **without an app update** — so most Google
  drift (a moved `pb`, endpoint, or field index) is now a one-line edit + version
  bump, not a release.
- ✅ **Signed update channel** — the bundle is **ECDSA-P256/SHA-256 signed** (`calibration.json.sig`); the app verifies it against a **pinned public key** before adopting, so a repo/CDN compromise can't push config — or code — to devices (private key kept out of the repo; `scripts/sign-calibration.sh` re-signs; `BundleSignature.verify` is unit-tested)
- ✅ **Pushed notices** — a `notices` array in the signed bundle surfaces dismissable alerts on the bare map ("search is down, fix coming") with **no app update**; dismissals persist per-id
- ✅ **Remote parse logic** (phase 3) — a signed `transformsJs` bundle runs in a **Rhino sandbox** (interpreted, no Java access), so a *response-shape* change can be hot-fixed too, not just a moved field. Two search hooks (`parseSearch` full re-parse / `transformPlaces` post-process) over a flat place-JSON contract; **compiled Kotlin is always the fallback**. Engine + sandbox + contract unit-tested
